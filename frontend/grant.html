Grant Information

The BOP Digital Platform was originally developed as a part of the Billion Oyster Project Curriculum and Community Enterprise for Restoration Science (BOP-CCERS). This project aimed to improve STEM education in public schools by linking teaching and learning to ecosystem restoration and engaging students in hands-on environmental field science during their regular school day. The project also strongly emphasized the use of computer science and digital tools for environmental monitoring, community science, stewardship, and advocacy. BOP-CCERS was a research-based partnership initiative between:

  • New York Harbor Foundation,
  • Pace University,
  • New York City Department of Education,
  • Columbia University Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory,
  • New York Academy of Sciences,
  • University of Maryland Center for Environmental Science,
  • New York Aquarium,
  • The River Project
  • Good Shepherd Services.
